Jeopardy vs. attack-defense

Jeopardy-style CTFs present categories (crypto, web, forensics) with independent challenges you solve for points. Attack-defense adds live networks: you harden a service while opponents probe it—closer to real-time security operations.

[✓]

Why it matters

CTFs build pattern recognition, tool fluency, and teamwork. They bridge lecture concepts and industry scenarios—resume-ready proof that you can think under pressure and document findings.

{*}

Skill areas

Typical tracks include cryptography, web exploitation, forensics, reverse engineering, OSINT, and binary analysis—each reinforcing the mindset: observe, hypothesize, verify, report.
